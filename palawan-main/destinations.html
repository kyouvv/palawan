San Vicente

Home to the famous Long Beach, stretching over 14 kilometers and considered the longest white-sand beach in the Philippines. San Vicente offers a quieter alternative to El Nido while showcasing pristine coastlines, island hopping adventures, waterfalls, and untouched natural beauty.

Taytay

Taytay is the former capital of Palawan and home to the historic Fort Santa Isabel, a Spanish fortress built in 1667. Visitors can enjoy rich cultural heritage, scenic bays, mangrove forests, and authentic local experiences away from crowded tourist spots.

Heritage Learn More →

Balabac

Located at the southernmost tip of Palawan, Balabac is known for crystal-clear turquoise waters, pink sand beaches, sandbars, marine sanctuaries, and untouched islands. Often called the Maldives of the Philippines, it remains one of the country's hidden gems.

Beach Learn More →

Narra

Known as the Rice Granary of Palawan, Narra offers waterfalls, rivers, caves, and lush forests. It is an excellent destination for eco-tourism, hiking, and experiencing the province's rich agricultural landscape.
